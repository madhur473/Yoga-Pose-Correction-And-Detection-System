Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

This fundamental pose improves posture and balance. Stand with your feet together, arms at your sides, and weight evenly distributed on both feet. Engage your thighs and lift your chest. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Downward Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

A great pose for stretching and strengthening the entire body. Start on your hands and knees, lift your hips towards the ceiling, and straighten your legs. Keep your hands shoulder-width apart and feet hip-width apart. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Child's Pose (Balasana)

This restful pose helps to relax and stretch the back and hips. Kneel on the floor, sit back on your heels, and stretch your arms forward on the mat. Rest your forehead on the ground and hold for 1 to 3 minutes.

Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I)

This pose strengthens the legs, opens the hips, and builds stamina. Step one foot forward, bend your front knee, and raise your arms overhead. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Tree Pose (Vrikshasana)

Improve your balance and focus with this pose. Stand on one leg, place the sole of the other foot on your inner thigh, and bring your hands together in front of your chest. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana)

This pose strengthens the back and opens the chest. Lie on your back with your knees bent, feet flat on the floor, and lift your hips towards the ceiling. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)

Stretch your chest and strengthen your spine with this gentle backbend. Lie on your stomach, place your hands under your shoulders, and lift your chest off the ground. Hold for 15 to 30 seconds.

Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana)

This pose stretches the hamstrings and back. Sit with your legs extended in front of you, and reach for your feet, keeping your back straight. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Cat-Cow Pose (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana)

This dynamic sequence stretches the spine and relieves tension. Start on your hands and knees, alternate arching and rounding your back, and hold each position for a few seconds. Repeat for 1 to 2 minutes.

Triangle Pose (Trikonasana)

This pose strengthens the legs and stretches the hips, spine, chest, and shoulders. Stand with your feet wide apart, turn one foot out, and reach one hand toward the floor while the other hand reaches up. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Garland Pose (Malasana)

This squat pose opens the hips and stretches the ankles and lower back. Squat with your feet slightly wider than hip-width, bringing your palms together at your chest. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Hero Pose (Virasana)

This seated pose stretches the thighs and improves posture. Kneel on the floor, sit back on your heels, and rest your hands on your thighs. Hold for 1 to 3 minutes.
