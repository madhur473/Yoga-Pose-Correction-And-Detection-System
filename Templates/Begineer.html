Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

This fundamental pose improves posture and balance. Stand with your feet together, arms at your sides, and weight evenly distributed on both feet. Engage your thighs and lift your chest. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Downward Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

A great pose for stretching and strengthening the entire body. Start on your hands and knees, lift your hips towards the ceiling, and straighten your legs. Keep your hands shoulder-width apart and feet hip-width apart. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Child's Pose (Balasana)

This restful pose helps to relax and stretch the back and hips. Kneel on the floor, sit back on your heels, and stretch your arms forward on the mat. Rest your forehead on the ground and hold for 1 to 3 minutes.
