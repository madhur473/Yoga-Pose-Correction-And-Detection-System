Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)

This pose strengthens the legs, hips, and arms while improving concentration and stamina. Extend your arms parallel to the floor, step one foot back, and bend the front knee. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Eagle Pose (Garudasana)

This balancing pose stretches the shoulders, hips, and legs. Wrap one leg over the other, cross your arms, and focus on a point to maintain balance. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Crow Pose (Bakasana)

This arm balance strengthens the arms, wrists, and core. Squat down, place your palms on the floor, and balance your knees on the back of your arms. Hold for 10 to 30 seconds.

Extended Side Angle (Utthita Parsvakonasana)

This pose stretches the side of the body, strengthens the legs, and opens the hips. Bend your front knee, place your forearm on your thigh, and extend your top arm overhead. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Half Moon Pose (Ardha Chandrasana)

This balancing pose strengthens the legs and opens the hips and chest. Stand on one leg, reach the other leg back, and extend one hand towards the ground while the other reaches up. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Boat Pose (Navasana)

This core-strengthening pose engages the abdominal muscles and spine. Sit on the floor, lift your legs off the ground, and extend your arms forward. Hold for 30 to 60 seconds.

King Pigeon Pose (Kapotasana)

This deep backbend and hip-opener increases flexibility in the hips, spine, and shoulders. From a lunge position, bend the back knee, and reach back to hold the foot. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute on each side.

Wheel Pose (Urdhva Dhanurasana)

This challenging backbend strengthens the back, arms, and legs. Lie on your back, bend your knees, place your hands by your ears, and push up into a full arch. Hold for 15 to 30 seconds.

Headstand (Sirsasana)

This inversion improves circulation and strengthens the core, shoulders, and arms. Begin by placing your forearms on the floor, interlacing your fingers, and balancing your legs in the air. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Dancer's Pose (Natarajasana)

This balancing backbend improves strength and flexibility. Stand on one leg, lift the opposite leg behind you, and reach back with your hand to hold your ankle. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Forearm Plank (Phalakasana)

This core-strengthening pose engages the abdominal muscles, shoulders, and legs. Place your forearms on the floor, lift your body into a straight line, and hold for 30 to 60 seconds.

Firefly Pose (Tittibhasana)

This advanced arm balance improves strength and flexibility in the arms, shoulders, and hamstrings. Place your hands on the floor, lift your legs over your arms, and extend them forward. Hold for 10 to 30 seconds.
