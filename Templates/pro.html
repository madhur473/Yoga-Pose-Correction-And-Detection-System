Handstand (Adho Mukha Vrksasana)

This inversion strengthens the shoulders, arms, and core while improving balance. Kick your legs up into a handstand, keeping your arms straight. Hold for 10 to 30 seconds.

Peacock Pose (Mayurasana)

This advanced arm balance strengthens the wrists, forearms, and core. Place your palms on the floor, engage your core, and lift your legs off the ground. Hold for 10 to 20 seconds.

Scorpion Pose (Vrschikasana)

This deep backbend improves flexibility and strength in the spine, arms, and shoulders. From a forearm stand, bend your legs towards your head. Hold for 10 to 30 seconds.

Lotus Headstand (Padmasana Sirsasana)

This advanced inversion combines the benefits of lotus pose and headstand, improving flexibility and core strength. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Side Crow (Parsva Bakasana)

This balancing pose strengthens the arms and obliques while improving focus and stability. Twist your body, place your hands on the floor, and lift your legs. Hold for 20 to 30 seconds.

Full Splits (Hanumanasana)

This deep stretch improves flexibility in the hips and hamstrings. Slide one leg forward and the other leg back into a full split. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Advanced Firefly Pose (Tittibhasana Variation)

This variation of Firefly Pose deepens the stretch in the hamstrings and strengthens the arms and core. Extend your legs even farther and lift your hips higher. Hold for 10 to 30 seconds.

One-Legged King Pigeon Pose (Eka Pada Rajakapotasana)

This deep backbend opens the hips, shoulders, and spine. From a pigeon pose, bend your back knee, reach back with your arms, and grab your foot. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Forearm Balance (Pincha Mayurasana)

This challenging inversion strengthens the arms, shoulders, and core. Begin in a forearm plank, kick your legs up, and balance on your forearms. Hold for 30 to 60 seconds.

Standing Split (Urdhva Prasarita Eka Padasana)

This pose increases flexibility in the hamstrings and hips while challenging your balance. Stand on one leg and lift the other leg as high as possible. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Tiger Pose (Vyaghrasana)

This pose strengthens the back, arms, and core while stretching the spine. From all fours, lift one leg up and back, arching your back. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Flying Pigeon Pose (Eka Pada Galavasana)

This advanced arm balance challenges flexibility and strength in the hips and core. Balance on your arms while bringing one leg into a pigeon position. Hold for 20 to 30 seconds on each side.
